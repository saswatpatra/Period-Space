Period Basics

What is a period? ‚ñº

A period, or menstruation, is the regular discharge of blood and mucosal tissue from the inner lining of the uterus through the vagina. It's part of the menstrual cycle that prepares the body for pregnancy each month.

Periods typically start between ages 10-16 (this is called menarche) and continue until menopause (usually between ages 45-55).

The Menstrual Cycle Explained ‚ñº

The average menstrual cycle lasts 28 days, but can range from 21 to 35 days in adults. The cycle has four phases:

  • Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5): The uterine lining sheds, resulting in period bleeding.
  • Follicular Phase (Days 6-14): The pituitary gland releases FSH, stimulating egg development.
  • Ovulation (Around Day 14): An egg is released from the ovary.
  • Luteal Phase (Days 15-28): The uterine lining thickens in preparation for pregnancy.
Common Period Products ‚ñº

There are several options for managing menstrual flow:

  • Pads: Adhesive strips worn in underwear to absorb flow
  • Tampons: Inserted into the vagina to absorb flow internally
  • Menstrual Cups: Reusable silicone cups inserted to collect flow
  • Period Underwear: Special absorbent underwear
  • Reusable Cloth Pads: Washable fabric pads

It's important to change products regularly (every 4-8 hours) to prevent infection.

Managing Period Symptoms ‚ñº

Common symptoms and ways to manage them:

  • Cramps: Use heat pads, gentle exercise, or over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Bloating: Reduce salt intake, stay hydrated, and eat potassium-rich foods
  • Mood swings: Practice stress-reduction techniques and get enough sleep
  • Fatigue: Maintain iron levels through diet and consider supplements if needed

If symptoms are severe (heavy bleeding, extreme pain), consult a healthcare provider as this could indicate conditions like endometriosis or PCOS.

Period Health & Hygiene ‚ñº

Maintaining good hygiene during your period is essential:

  • Wash your genital area regularly with water (avoid harsh soaps)
  • Change menstrual products frequently (every 4-8 hours)
  • Wash hands before and after changing products
  • Dispose of used products properly (wrap pads/tampons before throwing away)
  • Clean menstrual cups thoroughly between uses
  • Wear clean, breathable underwear

What Should Men Do?

Understanding Periods ‚ñº

As a man, understanding menstruation is the first step to being supportive. Remember:

  • Periods are a natural biological process, not something to be ashamed of
  • Every person's experience is different - some have mild symptoms, others severe pain
  • Hormonal changes can cause mood swings, fatigue, and other symptoms
  • It's not "just about bleeding" - there are many physical and emotional effects
